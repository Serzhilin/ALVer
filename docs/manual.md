**ALVer**  
**What is ALVer?**  
ALVer makes general assembly meetings simple, transparent, and independent from the Big Tech. Members can pre-register, give a mandate to someone else if they can't attend, check in at the door by scanning a QR code, and vote live from their phone. Facilitators run the entire meeting from a single screen: announcements, attendance, live polls, and a permanent archive of decisions. All data stays in your cooperative's own sovereign data space, not on a private server  on a different continent.

**For Attendees**  
**Before the meeting**  
When a meeting is announced you'll receive a link with three options:  
- **I'll come** — registers your intention to attend. You'll be on the expected list.  
- **I can't make it, give a mandate** — you can't attend but want your vote to count. Enter the name of a member who will be present; they will vote on your behalf for the whole meeting or specific agenda items.  
- **I can't make it** — lets the facilitator know you won't be there, no mandate needed.  
You can change your choice at any time before the meeting starts.  
**Checking in**  
At the venue, scan the QR code on the screen with your phone camera — no app needed. Your name will appear on the check-in screen with a welcome message. If there's no QR code handy, the facilitator can check you in manually.  
*If the meeting has already started and you weren't checked in, ask the facilitator to add you — they can add attendees at any point during the session.*  
**During the meeting — voting**  
When a poll opens, your phone screen automatically shows the question and the vote options. Tap your choice and confirm. You'll see a live progress bar showing how many members have voted. Once the poll closes, the result (Adopted / Rejected) is shown immediately on everyone's screen.  
If you hold a mandate from another member, you'll cast two votes: your own and theirs. The app will guide you through both.  
**Aspirant members** can attend and follow the meeting but cannot vote.  
**After the meeting**  
Once the facilitator closes the meeting, an archive becomes available with a full overview: who attended, all decisions with vote counts, and the time each poll was closed. You can access it from the meeting link at any time.

**For Facilitators**  
**Announcing a meeting**  
From your dashboard, create a new meeting and fill in:  
- **Name** — e.g. "General Assembly March 2026"  
- **Date, time, and location**  
- **Agenda** — paste the agenda text; members will see it when they register  
Click **Announce** to publish it. Members can now pre-register and give mandates.  
**Opening the session**  
On the day of the meeting, go to your dashboard and click **Open meeting**. This starts the live session. The display screen (TV/projector) will show a QR code for check-in and the live attendance count.  
*Use * ***📺 Open screen*** * to open the display view on a separate browser tab or device connected to the projector.*  
**Managing attendance**  
The **Attendees** tab shows everyone who has checked in, with a count of eligible voters and mandates. You can:  
- **Add without app** — check in a member who doesn't have their phone  
- **Add mandate** — record a mandate between two members on the spot  
- **Remove** an attendee if added by mistake  
The number of eligible voters updates in real time as people check in.  
**Running a poll**  
In the **Polls** tab, create a poll before or during the meeting:  
1. Enter the **motion text** (the question members will vote on)  
2. Add **vote options** — by default For / Against / Abstain, but you can customise  
3. Click **▶ Start poll** — the poll goes live immediately on all attendee screens  
4. Watch the progress bar; when enough members have voted click **⏹ Close poll**  
5. The result (Adopted / Rejected) is shown to everyone automatically  
You can prepare multiple polls in advance and start them one by one. Only one poll can be live at a time.  
If a member doesn't have their phone, use **📝 Add vote** to enter their vote manually.  
**Closing the meeting**  
Click **🔒 Close meeting** when all business is done. This finalises the archive. Members can access the full record of decisions from their meeting link.  
To reopen the meeting if needed, use **↩ Reopen meeting**.

**Privacy & data**  
ALVer is built on the W3DS (Web 3 Data Space) architecture. Your cooperative's data — meetings, votes, members — is stored in your own sovereign eVault, not on a centralised platform. Identity verification uses the eID app; no passwords, no accounts to manage.
